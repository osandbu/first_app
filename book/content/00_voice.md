# Voice and Style Guide

Write like a management thinker, not a technical author.
- Synthesize ideas across engineering, incentives, organizational behavior, finance, psychology, and strategy
- Avoid explaining transformer architectures; explain why AI changes how companies make decisions
- Ground arguments in enduring principles—decision-making, organizational design, incentives—not specific tools or models
- Use concrete examples from business history and organizational behavior
- Be direct and opinionated; this is a point of view, not a survey
- Write for senior executives who are smart but not technical
- Avoid tool names, model names, company-specific AI products—these date the book
- Prefer "AI systems" over specific products; prefer principles over features
- Chapters should feel like essays: a clear argument, evidence, implication
- Target 3,000-5,000 words per chapter
